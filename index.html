Abstract

We present Sarc7, a sarcasm benchmark with fine-grained subtype labels and emotion-informed annotations. It supports multi-task evaluation for both classification and generation. Our models demonstrate strong performance boosts from emotional context and subtype guidance.

Related Work

Prior sarcasm research has largely focused on binary detection with limited interpretability. Sarc7 extends this space by introducing structured subtype definitions and emotional grounding to support more expressive modeling.

Benchmark Features

  • 7 sarcasm types: hyperbole, rhetorical, understatement, etc.
  • Emotion-informed span annotations
  • Gold and silver label tiers
  • Multi-reference sarcastic generations

Methodology

We fine-tune transformer-based architectures (BERT, RoBERTa) for sarcasm detection and generation tasks. Emotion embeddings are integrated alongside subtype classification heads. Our pipeline incorporates both gold-annotated and distantly-labeled samples to support robustness.
